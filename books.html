Book Adaptations

There are many books based on Shakespeare's works. Some are direct adaptations; others use his characters and creations to tell us about a different place and time.

Book Riot is a good place to find others.

This verse novel finds some of Shakespeare's young heroines - Juliet, Lavinia, Ophelia, and Cordelia - aiding each other as they work through the intense things that they experienced while alive.

Rival gangs in 1920s Shanghai - and former loves, Roma and Juliette - are forced to work together to confront a monster in their midst.

In Hamnet, Maggie O'Farrell tells the imagined story of Hamnet, Shakespeare's son and namesake for Hamlet, and Anne Hathaway, Shakespeare's wife. It's full of beautiful descriptions of being a woman in 16th century England.

A retelling of Hamlet, with Hanalee investigating what happened to her Black father, who was married to Hanalee's White mother at a time when society and the Ku Klux Klan would not accept an interracial relationship - at any costs.
