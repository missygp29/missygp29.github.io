Movie Adaptations

The Taming of the Shrew is a vicious play, and this version softens it and allows us to root thoroughly for Kat and Patrick.

This production is... not without its issues; it's worth examining what it says about race. Without ignoring that, this version also captures the headiness of their love in a way that not many others manage.

Twelfth Night gets a reboot in this aughts classic, where Viola pretends to be a boy to play on a boys' soccer team.

If older classics and directors are more your thing, Kurosawa's adaptation of Macbeth has been called the best Shakespeare movie adaptation ever. Kurosawa also made a version of King Lear, called Ran.

This is a gory, disturbing, and incredibly designed adaptation (as you'd expect from the designer of The Lion King on Broadway) of a rarely-performed play about violence and revenge.
