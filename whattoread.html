Shakespeare in His Infinite Variety

What to Read

So, you're going to read some Shakespeare. There are so many versions! Scholars swear by the Arden versions, but they can be intimidating (so. many. notes.). If you can get Pelican versions, go for it! (You should be able to get some from your library or secondhand online at places like Thrift Books or Abe Books); Folger has free versions!

But which plays? And how? Here are a few ways you could approach it:

  • Choose plays with filmed versions - films of theater performances, movies of the original play, or, my preference, updates.
  • Read aloud a play with friends! It might feel weird to ask them to do it, but a read aloud can help you get the feel of it. And you can help each other get the language.
  • Find a challenge buddy to read some or all of the plays! You can get a collected works and read them all individually and compare. The Storygraph has a readalong with friends feature. One suggested order is chronologically: the Wikipedia version works!

  • And, for a really big challenge, some people read them all in a year! It takes about 15-20 minutes of reading per day, allegedly. If that seems intriguing, check out this Folger article about it and the schedule.
  • You could ignore the plays and read the poems instead
